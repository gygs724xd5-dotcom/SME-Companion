# Vision

SME Companion exists because small business owners do not need another empty dashboard, generic chatbot, or complicated CRM. They need a practical companion that understands their business, remembers what matters, and helps them decide what to do next.

Most SME owners operate with limited time, incomplete data, and constant pressure. They manage sales, content, customers, stock, staff, promotions, and daily problems at the same time. Many software tools ask them to become analysts before they can get value. SME Companion starts from a different belief: the system should meet the owner where they are, in normal language, with useful guidance.

## The Problem

Small businesses often have scattered knowledge:

- Store details live in the owner's head.
- Customer questions happen in chat apps.
- Content ideas are created manually.
- Sales goals are informal.
- Feedback is forgotten after a conversation.
- Product and business decisions are made without a durable memory.

SME Companion is built to collect those signals, organize them, and turn them into practical action.

## How It Differs From ChatGPT

ChatGPT is a powerful general assistant. SME Companion is a business-specific companion.

The difference is context and continuity. SME Companion knows the store profile, business type, recent topics, goals, generated content history, demo context, product feedback signals, and local memory structures. It uses deterministic business logic where possible and LLM responses where helpful.

The goal is not to replace a general-purpose model. The goal is to wrap AI in a product experience that understands SME work.

## How It Differs From Traditional CRM

A CRM usually tracks customers, pipelines, and sales activity. That is useful, but many small businesses do not start with structured customer operations.

SME Companion starts with the owner's daily needs:

- What should I post?
- What promotion should I run?
- What is wrong with my business right now?
- What should I improve next?
- How do I answer this situation?
- What product feedback are users giving us?

It is not trying to be a heavy database first. It is trying to become a helpful business brain first.

## How It Differs From Dashboard Software

Dashboards show data. SME Companion should explain what the data means and suggest what to do.

Traditional dashboards often assume the user already knows which metric matters. SME Companion is designed to interpret business context, detect missing signals, surface urgency, and translate insight into action.

## Core Philosophy

SME Companion is guided by a few product principles:

- Start with the business owner's language.
- Keep the experience practical and action-oriented.
- Use deterministic logic for reliability.
- Use LLMs for judgment, explanation, and conversation.
- Remember useful context over time.
- Turn feedback into product learning.
- Prefer simple operating workflows over complex configuration.

## Business Brain

The Business Brain understands the store profile, business type, recent content, goals, promotions, sales strategy, diagnosis, insights, and operating state.

Its purpose is to help owners see what is happening in the business and choose the next useful action.

## Conversation Brain

The Conversation Brain interprets what the user is asking, decides whether business context is needed, handles follow-up, remembers session context, and keeps responses clean and relevant.

Its purpose is to make SME Companion feel less like a form and more like an advisor.

## Product Brain

The Product Brain listens to feedback about SME Companion itself. When users complain, ask for features, mention confusion, or describe missing functionality, the system can classify the feedback, store it, prioritize it, merge duplicates, and surface trends.

Its purpose is to make the product learn from real usage.

## Future AI Product Manager

The future AI Product Manager will build on the Product Brain. Instead of only collecting feedback, it should help decide what to build next.

It should be able to identify repeated pain points, summarize product opportunities, recommend sprint priorities, draft release notes, and explain why one improvement matters more than another.

## Long-Term Direction

The long-term direction is a Business Operating System for SMEs: a product that combines memory, conversation, business guidance, product learning, and future integrations into a single practical companion.

The best version of SME Companion is not a chatbot sitting beside the business. It is a system that gradually becomes the business's working memory.
